How Do I Start Up?

Pitching

Pitching is related to storytelling. It's best to start there and craft a compelling story. Then distill that story into a clear, concise pitch that captures the most important elements.

Constructing the pitch

  • Six Slides is Fred Wilson's strong recommendation to construct a pitch deck with only six slides.

    1. Simplify your company's story into six killer slides
    2. Shorten the pitch deck so it leaves time for conversation
    3. Inspire investors so they ask follow up questions where you dive into further details

  • What story are you trying to tell to potential investors? is a post by Gabriel Weinberg that with four topics that should specifically be in a pitch to nail it.

  • Constructing a better startup pitch is another Weinberg post with a list of recommendations for answering the big "why's" that investors will be asking about whether or not to invest in you.

  • How To Pitch Your Tech Project To Us provides insight from a journalist about what is and is not successful with startup pitches.

  • Be genuine by telling your own unique story

  • Have the story "go behind the curtain" and show what really goes on instead of trying to present a perfect squeeky clean image
