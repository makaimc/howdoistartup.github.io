Pitching

Pitching is related to storytelling. It's best to start there and craft a compelling story. Then distill that story into a clear, concise pitch that captures the most important elements.

Constructing the pitch

If raising capital is necessary for your company then pitching to venture capitalists will be important. The following resources provide recommendations for storytelling, simplification and inspiration.

Six Slides

Fred Wilson's strong recommendation is to construct a pitch deck with only six slides.

  1. Simplify your company's story into six killer slides
  2. Shorten the pitch deck so it leaves time for conversation
  3. Inspire investors so they ask follow up questions where you dive into further details

How To Pitch Your Tech Project To Us

provides insight from a journalist about what is and is not successful with startup pitches.

  1. Be genuine by telling your own unique story
  2. Have the story "go behind the curtain" and show what really goes on instead of trying to present a perfect squeeky clean image
  3. The story should end with a "pot of gold" - a new algorithm, way of thinking or novel insight

Improving your pitch

The job is not done once you have the pitch deck created. Pitch, get feedback, tweak and iterate.

3 Ways to Improve Your Pitch Deck

This article argues that every pitch deck should at least answer these three questions:

  1. Does it look like your customer base is growing? Visually prove you are growing.
  2. Do your customers like your product? Acquiring new customers is more expensive than having existing customers stay customers.
  3. Does it look like your business/product can actually scale? VCs want to invest in businesses that grow to $100M+ within five years.
